Remark 3.7. (Moment matrices and SDP parameters) 

A linear functional admits an operator on the multivariate polynomial ring. 

When we fix the Chebyshev basis as a basis for the polynomial ring, the operator's matrix entries are expressed in the moments of the functional. 

Similarly, we can localized the operator at the Hermite matrix, which describes the image of the generlaized cosines. 

Truncation gives two finite matrices: 

> Type,n,d := 'B',2,2; #type, dimension, order of relaxation
d_min:=RowDimension(THermiteMatrix(Type,n)); #minimal order of relaxation
TMomentMatrix(Type,n,d); #truncated moment matrix with Chebyshev moments y
TLocalizedMomentMatrix(Type,n,d-d_min); #truncated localized moment matrix with Chebyshev moments y
 

 

 

 

Type,n,d≔B,2,2
d_min≔2
(4.1)
 

 

The size of the truncated (localized) moment matrix and the number of moments can be computed as follows. 

It depends on the type of the root system, the dimension and the order of the relaxation. 

> Type,n := 'B',2; #type, dimension
d_min:=RowDimension(THermiteMatrix(Type,n)); #minimal order of relaxation
for d from d_min to 10 do #size of the truncated and truncated localized moment matrix and number of matrices
nops([seq(op(ChebyshevLevel(Type,n,i)),i=0..d)]),
d_min*nops([seq(op(ChebyshevLevel(Type,n,i-d_min)),i=d_min..d)]),
nops([seq(op(ChebyshevLevel(Type,n,i)),i=0..2*d)])-1
od;
